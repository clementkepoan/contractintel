# what are the risks in this contract?

## Answer
Based on the provided contractual evidence, there are several key risks associated with this construction contract:

1. **Delayed Performance and Termination Risks:**
   - The contract includes strict penalties for delays. According to Clause 8 (S1/C1/C4/C6/C9), if the contractor fails to complete the work or make corrections within the specified time, a penalty of one per thousand of the total contract price will be imposed daily from the date of delay until termination or cancellation of the contract.
   - If the contractor breaches the contract and is notified in writing but still fails to fulfill their obligations, the client has the right to terminate the contract immediately (C1/C9). The contractor must refund a proportionate amount of the fees already received, and if additional damages are incurred by the client, the contractor remains liable for compensation.
   - Clause 8 also states that the penalty cap is equal to the total contract price, but this does not preclude the client from seeking other forms of damages (C1/C6).

2. **Payment Suspension Risks:**
   - The client has the right to withhold payments if they believe it will satisfy their claims (C2). This can significantly impact cash flow for the contractor.

3. **Contract Termination and Cancellation Risks:**
   - The contract allows for termination or cancellation due to breaches by the contractor, including delays in performance (C1/C9).

4. **Alternative Contractor Risks:**
   - If the client terminates the contract due to the contractor's breach, they are entitled to engage another company to complete the work and may require the original contractor to refund a proportion of the fees already paid (C1/C9).

5. **Damage Compensation Risks:**
   - The contractor is responsible for any damages caused by their actions or those of subcontractors (C3). If the client incurs contractual or non-contractual liabilities due to the contractor's failure, these must be borne by the contractor (C2/C5).

6. **Non-Transferability and Non-Divisibility Risks:**
   - The contract explicitly prohibits subcontracting without prior written consent from the client (C3). Breach of this clause can result in a penalty of 20% of the total contract price, which may be deducted from any payments due to the contractor.

7. **Force Majeure and Exclusion of Tariff Risks:**
   - The force majeure clause does not include tariffs as an excluded event (C10). This means that if tariffs increase significantly, it could still impact the contractor's obligations under the contract.
   
8. **Client Responsibility Transfer Risks:**
   - If the client breaches the contract and causes damage to third parties or incurs liabilities, these must be borne by the contractor (C2/C5).

9. **No Price Adjustment Risks:**
   - The total price is fixed and cannot be adjusted for any reason, including changes in material costs, exchange rates, or other factors (C8). This means that if there are unexpected increases in costs due to market fluctuations, the contractor must absorb these without additional compensation.

In summary, the primary risks in this contract include significant financial penalties for delays, potential suspension of payments, the risk of contract termination and having to refund fees, damage compensation obligations, restrictions on subcontracting, and the inability to adjust prices despite changes in costs. These risks are detailed in multiple clauses across the document (C1, C2, C3, C8, C9, C10).
